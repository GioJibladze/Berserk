BERSERK

1997

The Golden Age Arc - A Tale of Ambition, Betrayal, and Sacrifice

Anime Review

Today we will be reviewing the anime Berserk 1997. The popular manga by legendary mangaka Kentaro Miura has been animated many times, but it is the 1997 version that stands out as the best adaptation to date. The story follows Guts, a lone mercenary who encounters a group of mercenaries called the Band of the Hawk.

Their charismatic leader Griffith convinces him to join them after besting him in a duel twice. As Griffith drives the Hawks towards his dreams will Guts realise his own or will tragedy strike? At least it is true that man has no control, even over his own will.

Main Characters

Guts

10/10

Guts is a character that is heavily associated with grim culture and brand of sacrifice tattoos these days, but there is so much more to his character. Born from the womb of his dead mother, Guts was cursed from the very moment he drew breath from this world. As his life continued, every moment of ease was just another tragedy in disguise waiting to happen. Guts has no vision of a future but he still continues to fight.

Why is that? After meeting the band of the Hawk and Griffith, he temporarily found a place of belonging but this once again turned into another painful lesson. Do bonds make us weaker and cause us nothing but pain in the long run or is Guts just simply unlucky? Guts has suffered more than most anime characters but he still shows resilience and digs deep whenever faced with an impossible trial.

His nihilistic mindset doesn't stop him from fighting and protecting those around him. Guts isn't a good man but he still tries his best to do what is right, like so many of us today.

"WHY DO I ALWAYS SEE THESE THINGS...AFTER THEY'RE DONE AND GONE?"

A 9.6 for my second favourite anime character of all time! I could honestly talk about Guts for hours and write a much more in depth analysis of him but that's for another day. Guts provides alot of us hope in a twisted kind of way. He allows the readers to see that no matter how bad it gets, there is still a reason to keep fighting...even if we may not know what that reason is in the moment itself.

Griffith

9.8/10

Griffith stands as one of anime's most complex and compelling antagonists. Beautiful, charismatic, and utterly ruthless in pursuit of his dream, Griffith embodies the dangerous allure of absolute ambition. His relationship with Guts forms the emotional core of the series - a bond of friendship, rivalry, and ultimately betrayal that defines both characters.

What makes Griffith truly terrifying isn't his power, but his humanity. His dream of ruling his own kingdom drives every decision, and he's willing to sacrifice anything - and anyone - to achieve it. The Eclipse stands as one of the most shocking moments in anime history, where Griffith's true nature is finally revealed.

"I will have my own kingdom. I will not let anything stand in the way of that dream."

Griffith represents the seductive nature of power and ambition. His fall from grace is both inevitable and tragic, making him one of the most memorable villains in all of fiction.

Casca

9.2/10

Casca has only had eyes for Griffith since the day they met, but with the arrival of Guts her feelings begin to shift. A fierce warrior and the only female member of the Band of the Hawk, she initially resents Guts for disrupting the dynamic she treasured.

As the series progresses, Casca develops into one of the most tragic figures in the story. Her evolution from devoted follower to independent warrior to victim showcases the brutal reality of the world Miura created. Her relationship with both Guts and Griffith forms the emotional triangle that drives much of the series' drama.

"Griffith... is my reason for living. Without him, I am nothing."

Casca represents strength, vulnerability, and the devastating cost of being caught between two forces of nature. Her character arc is both empowering and heartbreaking.

Judeau

9.1/10

Judeau serves as the heart and conscience of the Band of the Hawk. A skilled knife-thrower and archer, he's also the most perceptive member of the group, often understanding the complex dynamics between his comrades better than they understand themselves.

His unrequited feelings for Casca and his loyalty to his friends make him one of the most sympathetic characters in the series. Judeau represents the common soldier caught up in the grand ambitions of others, yet he faces his fate with courage and dignity.

"I guess I was just a simple guy who wanted to protect the people he cared about."

Judeau's wisdom and selflessness make his fate all the more tragic. He embodies the best qualities of humanity in a world that seems determined to crush such virtues.

Skull Knight

9.4/10

The enigmatic Skull Knight stands as one of Berserk's most mysterious and powerful figures. This ancient warrior, bound in skeletal armor and mounted upon a spectral horse, serves as both harbinger of doom and unlikely savior throughout the series.

His cryptic warnings and timely interventions suggest a deep understanding of causality and fate. The Skull Knight represents the eternal struggle against the God Hand, having fought them for centuries. His relationship with Guts mirrors his own past tragedy, making him a mentor figure born from similar suffering.

What makes Skull Knight fascinating is his role as the "enemy of the God Hand's enemy." He operates on a level beyond mortal comprehension, wielding a sword forged from beherits and possessing knowledge that spans ages.

"In the deepest darkness, you will find your true strength."

Skull Knight embodies the concept of eternal resistance against cosmic evil. His presence adds a mythological weight to the series, representing hope in the face of seemingly insurmountable darkness.

Pippin

8.5/10

Pippin stands as the gentle giant of the Band of the Hawk. Despite his imposing physical presence and skill with heavy weapons, he possesses one of the kindest hearts in the entire series. His childlike innocence and genuine care for his comrades make him a beloved member of the group.

What makes Pippin special is his ability to find joy and wonder in simple things, even amidst the brutality of war. His friendship with Judeau and protective nature toward the other Hawks shows that strength isn't just about physical power, but about the courage to remain gentle in a harsh world.

His fate during the Eclipse serves as one of the most heartbreaking moments, as his innocence is violently stripped away by forces beyond his understanding.

"I just want everyone to be happy and safe."

Pippin represents the tragedy of innocence lost. His character reminds us that in Berserk's world, the purest hearts often suffer the cruelest fates, making his memory all the more precious.

Corkus

7.8/10

Corkus represents the ambitious commoner who dreams of rising above his station. As one of the original members of the Band of the Hawk, he's fiercely loyal to Griffith but harbors deep resentment toward Guts, whom he sees as a threat to his position and dreams of nobility.

His character serves as a mirror to common human flaws - jealousy, ambition, and the desire for recognition. While often abrasive and petty, Corkus's motivations are ultimately understandable. He represents the everyman caught up in extraordinary circumstances.

Despite his flaws, Corkus's loyalty to the Hawks and his genuine belief in Griffith's dream make him a complex character rather than a simple antagonist.

"I've followed Griffith this far, and I'll follow him to whatever end."

Corkus embodies the human struggle for significance and belonging. His jealousy and ambition make him flawed, but his dedication to the group shows the complicated nature of loyalty and friendship.

Rickert

8.7/10

Rickert stands as one of the few survivors of the Band of the Hawk, and his character development from a young, eager recruit to a mature craftsman represents hope for the future. His technical skills and engineering abilities make him unique among the Hawks.

What makes Rickert special is his growth throughout the series. He evolves from a starry-eyed youth who idolized Griffith to someone who can see through the facade and make difficult moral choices. His eventual confrontation with Griffith shows remarkable character development and moral courage.

As one of the few who remembers the Band of the Hawk as they truly were, Rickert serves as a living reminder of what was lost and what must be protected.

"I won't let their sacrifices be forgotten or meaningless."

Rickert represents growth, resilience, and the courage to stand up for what's right. His survival and development make him a beacon of hope in Berserk's dark world, showing that some can learn from tragedy without being destroyed by it.
